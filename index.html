Privacy Policy

Effective date: 22 July 2026 · Contact: firstmaster.studio@outlook.com

Ludo First (“the App”) is published by First Master Studio. This policy describes exactly what the App collects, where it goes, and how to get rid of it. It is written to match the answers given in the Google Play Data Safety form and the Apple App Privacy questionnaire.

1. Identity and account

The App signs you in anonymously. We never ask for, and never receive, an email address, a phone number, a password, or a social login. What we hold is a randomly generated Firebase user ID.

The only piece of information you write yourself is your nickname. It is visible to other players in lobbies, on the board, in quick chat and in friend requests, so please do not put personal information in it. Nicknames are screened automatically for offensive content before being saved.

3. What we do NOT collect

  • No free text. In-game chat sends only a fixed list of preset phrases. You cannot type a message, so no message content authored by you is transmitted.
  • No photos. If you set a custom profile picture, the image is stored only on your device and is never uploaded to our servers or shown to other players.
  • No blocked-players list on our servers. The players you block are stored on your device only. Blocking is per-device: reinstalling the App or switching phones clears the list.
  • No location, contacts, calendar, microphone, camera roll scanning, or payment details. Purchases are handled entirely by Google Play and the Apple App Store; we never see your card, address or billing information.

4. Purchases and virtual currency

In-app purchases are processed by Google Play Billing or Apple’s In-App Purchase system. We receive only a product identifier, a purchase identifier and the resulting coin amount. Coins, diamonds and cosmetic items are virtual goods with no monetary value: they cannot be exchanged, transferred outside the App, or redeemed for real money.

5. Advertising and consent

The App shows banner, interstitial and rewarded ads through Google AdMob, which may use your device’s advertising identifier.

  • In the EEA, the UK and Switzerland, a Google-certified consent dialog (UMP) appears before any personalized ad is served. You can decline personalized ads and still play the game normally.
  • On iOS, the system App Tracking Transparency prompt asks for your permission before any tracking identifier is used. Declining is fully supported.

6. Reporting and moderation

If you report a player, we record who reported whom, the room code, the preset message concerned and the time. These records are used only to review abuse and to act on repeat offenders.

Because accounts are anonymous and therefore trivial to recreate, moderation records are kept even after an account is deleted — otherwise deleting an account would erase any record of abusive behaviour. They contain identifiers and a preset phrase, never a free-text message or contact details.

7. Children

Ludo First is not directed at children under 13 (under 16 in the EEA). The App contains in-app purchases and ads, and includes coin-stake matches played with virtual currency. We do not knowingly collect data from children below these ages. If you believe a child has used the App, contact us at firstmaster.studio@outlook.com and we will delete the associated data.

8. Deleting your data

You can delete your account from Settings → Delete Account inside the App. This is immediate and irreversible. It removes:

  • your public profile (nickname, avatar, flag);
  • your wallet — coins, diamonds, XP and every cosmetic you own or bought;
  • your entire match history;
  • your friends list, including the entry that other players hold for you;
  • your pending friend requests, in both directions;
  • any matchmaking queue entry.

Two things are deliberately retained and are not linked to a nickname: moderation records (§6) and purchase records, the latter because they are what prevents the same purchase being delivered twice and because tax and anti-fraud obligations require them.

You may also write to firstmaster.studio@outlook.com to request deletion.

Game Rules

How Ludo First plays — the rules the game actually enforces.

Goal

Each player has four pawns starting in their yard. Bring all four of them around the board and into your home column to win.

Getting a pawn out

You need to roll a 6 to move a pawn out of your yard and onto your starting square. Rolling a 6 also earns you an extra roll.

Moving

  • Move one pawn by the number rolled. If no legal move exists, your turn passes.
  • Landing exactly on a square occupied by a single opposing pawn captures it: that pawn goes back to its yard, and you get an extra turn.
  • Entering the home column requires an exact roll. Overshooting is not a legal move.

Safe squares

Eight squares on the track are marked as safe. A pawn standing on one of them cannot be captured, and neither can a pawn still in its yard or already in a home column.

Blockades

Two or more of your pawns on the same square form a blockade. Opposing pawns cannot pass through or land on it.

The three-sixes rule

Rolling three 6s in a row forfeits your turn — the third 6 is void and play passes to the next player.

Team Up (2v2)

  • Players sitting opposite each other are teammates: seats 1 & 3 form one team, seats 2 & 4 the other.
  • There is no friendly fire — you cannot capture your teammate’s pawns.
  • Teammates share blockades, and the team wins together.
  • A player whose four pawns are all home keeps playing, moving their teammate’s pawns.

Turn timer

In online matches each turn is capped at 35 seconds. If it runs out, the game plays a move for you so the match does not stall for everyone else.
